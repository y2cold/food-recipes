Tibs is a delectable dish originating in Ethiopia, which is made with saut√©ed or stir-fried diced beef or lamb, onions, garlic, peppers, and tomatoes cooked with berbere spice and niter kibbeh. This traditional dish has been served in Ethiopia for centuries and is now enjoyed around the world. Whether it is served as a main course or as part of a larger meal, Tibs provides an unforgettable experience for all who eat it

Ingredients

  • 1kg beef
  • 2 chopped medium-sized onions
  • 2 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsp berbere
  • 5 tbsp kibbe (Ethiopian butter)

How to make Tibs (step-by-step)

  1. Start by melting the kibbe in a large-sized sauce pan with your stove set to medium heat. Then add the garlic and onions along with some berbere.
  2. Lower the heat of the stove to medium-low and continue cooking for around 30 minutes until the onions start to darken, while occasionally stirring.
  3. When the onions get to a low sizzle, move them to a food processor and blend them well.
  4. Add some salt.
  5. Bring your beef meat, cut it into small 1 inch cubes and add some salt to all sides of the beef cubes
